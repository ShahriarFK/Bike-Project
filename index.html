Our 3 rules- 1/Be visible at all times. - Wear bright and visible clothes. Reflective stripes ads viability. Always stay out of blind zones. Alert other drivers before entering their blind zone. 2/Be predictable. - Others on the road must be able to predict your next move. Give proper signals before changing a lane or taking deviation. 3/Everyone in the road is dumb and partially blind. - Well, just imagine so and be prepared for an unexpected turn or braking.

Before going on a long ride, take your bike around the block and make sure it is safe to ride. First of all, your tires are going to be deflated, it is normal after long periods of time on storage. Inflate them close to the recommended level, you can find that in the wall of the tire. Make sure your brakes work. First do it off your bike, make sure the brake pads make contact with the rim or the rotor appropriately. Then do it with while riding your bike around the block. Make sure you test it with enough speed, but do it safely. It is common for cables to stretch and for air to build up in hydraulic brakes.
